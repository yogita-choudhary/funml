Lecture 27: Uncertainty Quantification in Neural Networks

Solutions:

  1. (MC) Noise/distortions in acquisition are inherent data noise \(\Rightarrow\) \[\boxed{\text{(B) Aleatoric uncertainty}}\]

  2. (TF) Epistemic uncertainty is model/data knowledge uncertainty and can be reduced: \[\boxed{\text{True}}\]

  3. (MC) Dropout at test time + averaging stochastic passes: \[\boxed{\text{(B) Monte Carlo Dropout (MC-Dropout)}}\]

  4. (Numeric) \[H(\bar{p}) = -\left(0.70\ln 0.70 + 0.20\ln 0.20 + 0.10\ln 0.10\right)\] Compute terms: \[0.70\ln0.70 \approx 0.70(-0.3567)= -0.2497\] \[0.20\ln0.20 \approx 0.20(-1.6094)= -0.3219\] \[0.10\ln0.10 \approx 0.10(-2.3026)= -0.2303\] Sum: \[H(\bar{p}) \approx -\left(-0.2497-0.3219-0.2303\right)=0.8019\] Rounded: \[\boxed{H(\bar{p}) = 0.80}\]

  5. (MC) NLL and Brier Score are per-sample metrics: \[\boxed{\text{(A) Per-sample uncertainty metrics}}\]

Final answers: \[\boxed{(1)\;B,\quad (2)\;T,\quad (3)\;B,\quad (4)\;0.80,\quad (5)\;A}\]
