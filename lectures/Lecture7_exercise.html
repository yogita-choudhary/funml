Lecture 7: Linear Regression (Least Squares / Normal Equation)

Question 1

We use the linear regression model: \[\boldsymbol{\hat{y}} = \mathbf{X}\boldsymbol{\hat{\theta}}, \qquad \boldsymbol{\hat{\theta}} = (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1}\mathbf{X}^T \boldsymbol{y}\]

Given: Consider the following dataset with 3 data points and 3 features: \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 2 & 1\\ 1 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \boldsymbol{y} = \begin{bmatrix} 2\\ 3\\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\]

Tasks:

  1. Compute the transpose matrix \(\mathbf{X}^T\).

  2. Compute the matrix \(\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X}\).

  3. Compute the vector \(\mathbf{X}^T \boldsymbol{y}\).

  4. State the normal equation expression for the least squares solution \(\boldsymbol{\hat{\theta}}\).

  5. Compute the final least squares solution \(\boldsymbol{\hat{\theta}}\).

  6. Compute the predicted output for \[\boldsymbol{x}_{\text{new}} = \begin{bmatrix} 1\\ 1\\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \hat{y}_{\text{new}} = \boldsymbol{x}_{\text{new}}^T \boldsymbol{\hat{\theta}}.\]

Round all final numeric answers to 2 decimals. A calculator is allowed (and recommended).
