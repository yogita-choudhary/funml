Lecture 4: Logistic Regression & Support Vector Machines

Recall: \[s_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b), \qquad L(x_i,y_i,\theta)=\max(0,\;1-s_i).\]

Given: \[w=[0.6,-0.2],\qquad b=0.4\] \[x_1=[2,1],\; y_1=+1 \qquad\qquad x_2=[1,3],\; y_2=-1\]

Solutions:

  1. Compute \(s_i=y_i(w^T x_i+b)\)

    For \((x_1,y_1)\): \[w^T x_1 = 0.6(2)+(-0.2)(1)=1.2-0.2=1.0\] \[w^T x_1+b = 1.0+0.4=1.4\] \[s_1 = (+1)(1.4)=1.4\] \[\boxed{s_1=1.40}\]

    For \((x_2,y_2)\): \[w^T x_2=0.6(1)+(-0.2)(3)=0.6-0.6=0\] \[w^T x_2+b=0+0.4=0.4\] \[s_2 = (-1)(0.4)=-0.4\] \[\boxed{s_2=-0.40}\]

  2. Check the margin constraint: \(s_i-1\ge 0\)

    \[s_1-1 = 1.4-1=0.4\ge 0 \Rightarrow \text{constraint satisfied}\] \[\boxed{\text{$x_1$ satisfies margin constraint.}}\]

    \[s_2-1 = -0.4-1=-1.4<0 \Rightarrow \text{constraint violated}\] \[\boxed{\text{$x_2$ violates margin constraint.}}\]

  3. Compute margin-perceptron cost

    \[L_1=\max(0,1-s_1)=\max(0,1-1.4)=\max(0,-0.4)=0\] \[\boxed{L_1=0.00}\]

    \[L_2=\max(0,1-s_2)=\max(0,1-(-0.4))=\max(0,1.4)=1.4\] \[\boxed{L_2=1.40}\]

Final answers (2 decimals): \[\boxed{s_1=1.40,\; s_2=-0.40,\; L_1=0.00,\; L_2=1.40}\]
