Lecture 4: Logistic Regression & Support Vector Machines

In Lecture 4, we defined the SVM margin constraint: \[y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1 \ge 0, \quad y_i \in \{-1,+1\}\] and the margin-perceptron cost: \[L(x_i,y_i,\theta) = \max(0,\; 1 - y_i(w^T x_i + b)).\]

Given: \[w = [0.6,\,-0.2],\qquad b=0.4\] Two labeled samples: \[x_1=[2,\,1],\; y_1=+1 \qquad\qquad x_2=[1,\,3],\; y_2=-1\]

Tasks:

  1. Compute the signed margin quantity: \[s_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b) \quad \text{for } i=1,2.\]

  2. For each point, determine whether the margin constraint is satisfied: \[y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1 \ge 0.\]

  3. Compute the margin-perceptron cost \(L(x_i,y_i,\theta)\) for \(i=1,2\).

Round all final answers to 2 decimals.
