Lecture 10: Regression Model Validation and Clustering

Context: Bias–Variance Tradeoff (Regression Models)

The table below shows ground truth (GT) and predictions from three models on five samples:

\[\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c} \text{Sample} & \text{GT} & \text{Model 1} & \text{Model 2} & \text{Model 3}\\ \hline 1 & 5 & 4.8 & 5.2 & 5.0\\ 2 & 6 & 4.7 & 5.3 & 6.0\\ 3 & 7 & 4.9 & 5.5 & 7.2\\ 4 & 8 & 5.0 & 5.7 & 8.0\\ 5 & 9 & 5.2 & 5.9 & 9.3 \end{array}\]

We define: \[\text{Bias}^2 = \left(\mathbb{E}[\hat{f}(x)] - f(x)\right)^2, \qquad \text{Variance} = \mathbb{E}\left[(\hat{f}(x)-\mathbb{E}[\hat{f}(x)])^2\right].\]

Tasks:

  1. Compute the mean prediction of each model across the 5 samples.

  2. Using those means, compute the average Bias\(^2\) for each model: \[\text{AvgBias}^2 = \frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=1}^5\left(\bar{y}_{\text{model}} - \text{GT}_i\right)^2.\]

  3. Based on the values of bias and variance, is model 1 most likely underfitting or overfitting?

Round all final numerical answers to 2 decimals.
