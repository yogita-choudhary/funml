Lecture 24: Anomaly Detection

In anomaly detection, we consider binary outcomes (Anomaly vs Normal). Performance metrics: \[\text{TPR} = \frac{\text{TP}}{\text{TP}+\text{FN}}, \qquad \text{FPR} = \frac{\text{FP}}{\text{FP}+\text{TN}}\]

Given: A detector is tested on a dataset with: \[\text{\# Anomalies} = 20,\qquad \text{\# Normal} = 80\] At threshold \(\gamma_1\), the detector produces: \[\text{TP}=16,\quad \text{FP}=12\] At a higher threshold \(\gamma_2\), the detector produces: \[\text{TP}=12,\quad \text{FP}=4\]

Tasks:

  1. Compute \(\text{TPR}\) and \(\text{FPR}\) at threshold \(\gamma_1\).

  2. Compute \(\text{TPR}\) and \(\text{FPR}\) at threshold \(\gamma_2\).

  3. Which threshold is more conservative (fewer false positives)? (Answer: \(\gamma_1\) or \(\gamma_2\))

  4. (True/False) Increasing \(\gamma\) typically decreases both TPR and FPR.

  5. (Multiple choice) Which ROC point is closer to the ideal detector? \[A: (\text{FPR},\text{TPR}) = (0.15, 0.80) \qquad B: (\text{FPR},\text{TPR}) = (0.05, 0.60)\]

Round numeric answers to 2 decimals.

Canvas keywords (do NOT type full question in Canvas):

  • Q1: TPR, FPR at \(\gamma_1\) (TP=16, FP=12, A=20, N=80)

  • Q2: TPR, FPR at \(\gamma_2\) (TP=12, FP=4, A=20, N=80)

  • Q3: conservative threshold? (fewer FP)

  • Q4: T/F threshold \(\uparrow\) changes TPR/FPR

  • Q5: closer to ideal ROC? A vs B
