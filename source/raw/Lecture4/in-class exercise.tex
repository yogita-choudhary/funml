{\large \textbf{Lecture 4: Logistic Regression \& Support Vector Machines}}

\vspace{8pt}

In Lecture 4, we defined the SVM \textbf{margin constraint}:
\[
y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1 \ge 0,
\quad y_i \in \{-1,+1\}
\]
and the \textbf{margin-perceptron cost}:
\[
L(x_i,y_i,\theta) = \max(0,\; 1 - y_i(w^T x_i + b)).
\]

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent
\textbf{Given:}
\[
w = [0.6,\,-0.2],\qquad b=0.4
\]
Two labeled samples:
\[
x_1=[2,\,1],\; y_1=+1
\qquad\qquad
x_2=[1,\,3],\; y_2=-1
\]

\vspace{8pt}
\noindent
\textbf{Tasks:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*), itemsep=6pt]
    \item Compute the signed margin quantity:
    \[
    s_i = y_i(w^T x_i + b)
    \quad \text{for } i=1,2.
    \]
    \item For each point, determine whether the margin constraint is satisfied:
    \[
    y_i(w^T x_i + b) - 1 \ge 0.
    \]
    \item Compute the margin-perceptron cost $L(x_i,y_i,\theta)$ for $i=1,2$.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent
\textbf{Round all final answers to 2 decimals.}
