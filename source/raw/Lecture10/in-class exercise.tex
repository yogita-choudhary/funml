\noindent
\textbf{Context: Bias--Variance Tradeoff (Regression Models)}

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent
The table below shows ground truth (GT) and predictions from three models on five samples:

\vspace{4pt}
\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c|c}
\text{Sample} & \text{GT} & \text{Model 1} & \text{Model 2} & \text{Model 3}\\ \hline
1 & 5 & 4.8 & 5.2 & 5.0\\
2 & 6 & 4.7 & 5.3 & 6.0\\
3 & 7 & 4.9 & 5.5 & 7.2\\
4 & 8 & 5.0 & 5.7 & 8.0\\
5 & 9 & 5.2 & 5.9 & 9.3
\end{array}
\]

\vspace{8pt}
\noindent
We define:
\[
\text{Bias}^2 = \left(\mathbb{E}[\hat{f}(x)] - f(x)\right)^2,
\qquad
\text{Variance} = \mathbb{E}\left[(\hat{f}(x)-\mathbb{E}[\hat{f}(x)])^2\right].
\]

\vspace{10pt}
\noindent
\textbf{Tasks:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\arabic*), itemsep=6pt]
\item Compute the \textbf{mean prediction} of each model across the 5 samples.
\item Using those means, compute the \textbf{average Bias$^2$} for each model:
\[
\text{AvgBias}^2 = \frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=1}^5\left(\bar{y}_{\text{model}} - \text{GT}_i\right)^2.
\]
\item Based on the values of bias and variance, is \textbf{model 1} most likely \textbf{underfitting or overfitting}?
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{6pt}
\noindent
\textbf{Round all final numerical answers to 2 decimals.}
